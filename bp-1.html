Too Many Cyclic Quads to Handle - 2017 Polish MO, Final Round/P1

$28$th December, $2023$

  • geometry

Introduction

A very nice geometry problem that I came across while reading the Crux Monthly of May $2020$, apparantly it was problem posed at the Polish MO $2017$. The prerequisites mainly include the elementary results regarding cyclic quadrilaterals and other circle properties. Here I will be presenting a synthetic solution, and if you are further interested you can read the mentioned issue of Crux where you will find a non-synthetic solution using BaryCentric Coordinates proposed by Andrea Fanchini.

Problem

$($ Polish MO $2017$, Final Round $)$ Points $P$ and $Q$ lie respectively on sides $AB$ and $AC$ of a triangle $ABC$ such that $BP = CQ$. Segments $BQ$ and $CP$ intersect at $R$. The circumcircles of triangles $BPR$ and $CQR$ intersect again at point $S$ different from $R$. Prove that point $S$ lies on the angle bisector $\angle BAC$.

Solution

Let $PS$ and $BR$ intersect at $D$ and let $RC$ and $SQ$ intersect at $E$.

Claim 1. $DEQP, BSQA, SCAP$ are all cyclic quadrilaterals.
Proof. It's easy to see that $(PBR)$ and $(SCQ)$ are congruent and this implies, $DEQP$ is cyclic as $\angle RPD=\angle EQR$. Followed by $BS=SQ$ and $PS=SC$.

Now notice that $\angle SCA=\angle SPB$, which means $SCAP$ is cyclic. Similarly we can show that $BSQA$ is also cyclic. $\square$
We had previously shown that $\angle RPD=\angle EQR$ which concludes the fact that $S$ indeed lies on the angle bisector $\angle CAB$.$\square$
